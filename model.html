Disease Progression

How cataracts silently
steal your sight

Roughly 1 in 6 people will develop clinically significant cataract. Scroll to see how protein aggregation progressively degrades vision — and why early detection is decisive.

Stage 00 / Baseline
Healthy Vision
Crisp detail, accurate color, full contrast. The crystalline lens is transparent. Sharp focal clarity with no opacity in the lens structure.
Stage 01 / Early
Early Stage
Protein aggregates begin forming. Images appear slightly dull or dim. Often unnoticed by the patient. The optimal intervention window.
Stage 02 / Immature
Immature Cataract
Significant yellowing and contrast loss. Reading and driving become difficult. Protein aggregation increases intra-lenticular light scattering.
Stage 03 / Mature
Mature Cataract
Dense nuclear opacity. Vision is reduced to a blurred haze. Surgical lens replacement with IOL is the only effective treatment at this stage.

Interpretation Guide

How to read your result

Cataract Present
Clear imaging evidence of cataract. Opacity patterns consistent with clinically significant lens changes.
Clinical evaluation required
Likely Early Cataract
Early structural lens changes detected. Not yet advanced. Optimal window for preventive monitoring.
Routine monitoring advised
Likely Non-Cataract
No definitive cataract detected. Patterns may overlap with very early features or normal variation.
Monitor if symptoms appear
No Cataract Detected
Lens appearance consistent with healthy structure. No imaging evidence of cataract observed.
No follow-up required
